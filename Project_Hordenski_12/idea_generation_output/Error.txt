**Title: Anomaly and Symmetry Breaking in Quantum-Corrected Scalar-Tensor Gravity**

**Abstract:**
Symmetries play a foundational role in protecting the theoretical consistency of scalar-tensor theories. This paper investigates the fate of a proposed local symmetry in a higher-derivative gravity model when one-loop quantum corrections, represented by Gauss-Bonnet ($\beta_{\text{GB}}$) and Weyl-squared ($\beta_{W^2}$) terms, are included. The "classical" part of the action, containing specific Horndeski/DHOST-type operators, is constructed to be invariant under a transformation that mixes the scalar field and the metric, $\delta_\epsilon \phi = \epsilon(x) \Lambda$ and $\delta_\epsilon g_{\mu\nu} = \epsilon(x) \mathcal{L}_{\xi} g_{\mu\nu}$ where $\xi^\mu \propto \phi^\mu$.

The central question we address is whether this symmetry survives the inclusion of the curvature-squared corrections. Rather than assuming invariance, our primary objective is to perform a direct and rigorous calculation of the variation of the Gauss-Bonnet and Weyl-squared terms. We anticipate that the symmetry will be broken. The main contribution of this work will be the explicit derivation of the symmetry-breaking functional, or "anomaly," that arises from these terms. We will analyze the structure of this functional, which depends on higher derivatives of the fields and the gauge parameter $\epsilon(x)$. The form of this anomaly provides a direct measure of the extent to which quantum corrections violate the underlying classical symmetry, potentially reintroducing instabilities that the symmetry was supposed to eliminate. This investigation offers a concrete framework for studying how quantum effects can impact the fundamental structure and viability of modified gravity theories.
