**Disformal Equivalence and One-Loop Quantum Protection in Horndeski Theories**

This paper proposes a groundbreaking approach to establish one-loop ghost-freedom in a specific sub-sector of Horndeski theories by leveraging their disformal equivalence to simpler, demonstrably stable theories. The core idea is to:

1.  **Identify a "Disformally Equivalent Sub-sector":** Explicitly define a non-trivial sub-sector of the Horndeski Lagrangian that, through a precisely formulated disformal transformation of the metric and scalar field, can be mapped to a simpler theory (e.g., a canonical scalar field coupled to gravity or a known stable k-essence model). This disformal transformation serves as the explicit hidden symmetry.
2.  **One-Loop Stability via Equivalence:** Perform the one-loop perturbative analysis (in an FRW background) on the *disformally-mapped, simpler theory* to explicitly demonstrate the absence of ghosts and tachyons. The quantum stability of the original Horndeski sub-sector is then rigorously inferred from this disformal equivalence, providing a tractable method to address the complex one-loop calculations.
3.  **Galilean Symmetry and Observational Constraints:** Show how Galilean symmetry emerges as a special case within this disformal framework, particularly under specific conditions or choices of Horndeski functions. The identified quantum-protected Horndeski sub-sector will be thoroughly assessed for compatibility with observational constraints on gravitational wave speed, cosmological expansion, and structure growth.
4.  **Preventing Ghost Reappearance:** Argue how this disformal equivalence, by linking to a theory whose quantum stability is better understood or more easily verifiable at higher loop orders, provides a concrete mechanism for the "certain symmetries" discussed in Ref. 2004.11655 that prevent the reappearance of ghosts.
