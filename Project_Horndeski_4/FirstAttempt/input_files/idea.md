**Symmetry-Protected Horndeski: Unveiling Stability via Disformal-Generalized Galilean Co-invariance**

This paper proposes to rigorously investigate **Disformal-Generalized Galilean Co-invariance** as a fundamental hidden symmetry protecting a specific subclass of Horndeski theories from instabilities. The central hypothesis is that the simultaneous invariance under field-dependent disformal transformations of the metric and a generalized Galilean symmetry (GGS) for the scalar field acts as a robust mechanism guaranteeing the absence of ghosts and tachyons, extending to the one-loop perturbative level.

The paper will:
1.  **Explicitly derive the transformations** for both the metric and the scalar field under a novel GGS (a field-dependent extension of standard Galilean shifts) and disformal transformations.
2.  **Determine the precise conditions** on the Horndeski functions ($G_i$) that ensure *simultaneous invariance* under both transformations, thereby defining a specific, non-trivial subclass of Horndeski theories.
3.  **Demonstrate classical stability** for this subclass by showing explicit absence of ghosts and tachyons.
4.  **Address one-loop quantum stability** by showing how the algebraic structure imposed by Disformal-Generalized Galilean Co-invariance satisfies the criteria presented in https://arxiv.org/pdf/2004.11655, preventing the reappearance of ghost modes at the first-order perturbative level.
5.  **Investigate the extent to which this combined symmetry structure contributes to UV stability**, focusing on its role in maintaining a well-behaved effective field theory by suppressing problematic quantum corrections.
6.  **Explain why standard Galilean symmetry emerges as a special limiting case** of the proposed GGS.
7.  **Ensure compatibility with observational constraints** such as the speed of gravitational waves, cosmological expansion, and structure growth, by analyzing the specific conditions on the Horndeski functions imposed by these symmetries.

This work aims to characterize a novel class of stable and observationally viable Horndeski theories, elucidating the profound interplay between disformal transformations and generalized Galilean symmetries in safeguarding fundamental theoretical properties.
