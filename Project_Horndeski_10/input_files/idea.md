**Title:** Degeneracy as an Emergent Gauge Symmetry in the Perturbative Spectrum of Higher-Order Scalar-Tensor Theories

**Abstract:**
We propose a new physical principle for understanding the stability of degenerate higher-order scalar-tensor (HOST) theories. Instead of seeking a symmetry of the full non-linear action, we analyze the structure of the quadratic action for cosmological perturbations around a Friedmann-Lemaître-Robertson-Walker background. In a generic, non-degenerate theory, this action describes two propagating scalar modes, one of which is an Ostrogradsky ghost. We demonstrate that the imposition of the Type Ia degeneracy conditions makes the kinetic matrix for these scalar perturbations singular. Our central result is to show that this singularity is the hallmark of a previously unrecognized gauge symmetry at the level of the linearized theory. We explicitly construct the gauge transformation for the perturbation fields from the null eigenvector of the degenerate kinetic matrix and prove that this transformation leaves the quadratic action invariant. This reframes the degeneracy conditions: they are not merely ad-hoc algebraic constraints to eliminate a ghost, but are precisely the requirements for the theory's perturbative spectrum to be endowed with a symmetry that projects out the unphysical degree of freedom. This provides a direct physical interpretation of the health conditions for HOST theories in terms of the symmetries of their observable fluctuations.
