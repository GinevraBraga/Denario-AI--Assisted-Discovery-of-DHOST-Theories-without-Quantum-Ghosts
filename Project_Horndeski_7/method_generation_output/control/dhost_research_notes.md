# Research Questions, Hypotheses, and Working Assumptions

## Scope of the Study

This project is strictly focused on the analysis of Type I Degenerate Higher-Order Scalar-Tensor (DHOST) theories with massless gravity, as formulated in arXiv:2012.10218. The investigation is limited to the subclass of DHOST models that propagate only the two tensorial degrees of freedom of the graviton and a single scalar degree of freedom, with the graviton remaining massless. The Lagrangian under consideration is constructed from the general Type I DHOST action, parameterized by the functions \(A_1\) to \(A_5\), and is to be analyzed on a spatially flat Friedmann-Robertson-Walker (FRW) background.

## Instabilities to be Addressed

The primary instabilities targeted in this analysis are:
- **Ghost instabilities:** The presence of degrees of freedom with negative kinetic energy, leading to unbounded Hamiltonians and catastrophic vacuum decay.
- **Tachyonic instabilities:** The occurrence of fields with negative mass-squared, resulting in exponential growth of perturbations at low momenta.

Other potential pathologies (such as gradient instabilities or strong coupling) are not the main focus, as the ghost and tachyonic instabilities are the most severe and directly related to the higher-derivative structure of DHOST theories.

## Hypotheses on Hidden Symmetries

- **Existence of Protective Symmetries:** It is hypothesized that certain hidden symmetries, particularly shift symmetry (\(\phi \rightarrow \phi + c\)), can be imposed on the scalar sector of Type I DHOST theories to protect against the emergence of ghosts and tachyons, both at the classical and quantum (one-loop) levels.
- **Explicit Symmetry Transformations:** The analysis will seek explicit forms of variable transformations (including but not limited to shift symmetry) that leave the Lagrangian invariant and ensure the absence of pathological degrees of freedom.
- **Robustness of Symmetries:** The study will test whether these symmetries remain effective in the presence of small symmetry-breaking terms (approximate shift symmetry) and under quantum corrections, referencing the arguments in arXiv:2004.11655 regarding the possible reappearance of ghosts.

## Observational Constraints

The following observational constraints will be imposed on the theory and any proposed symmetries:
- **Speed of Gravitational Waves:** The propagation speed of tensor modes must match the speed of light, as required by multi-messenger astrophysical observations (e.g., GW170817).
- **Cosmological Expansion:** The background evolution must be compatible with the observed expansion history of the universe.
- **Structure Growth:** The theory must allow for structure formation consistent with large-scale structure surveys.

## Role of Shift Symmetry and Other Candidate Symmetries

- **Shift Symmetry:** The primary candidate symmetry is the shift symmetry of the scalar field, \(\phi \rightarrow \phi + c\), which restricts the functional dependence of the coupling functions \(A_1\)–\(A_5\) to derivatives of \(\phi\) only.
- **Additional Symmetries:** The analysis will remain open to the identification of further hidden symmetries, provided they can be explicitly constructed and shown to be compatible with the FRW background and observational constraints.

## Working Assumptions

- **Background Metric:** The analysis will be performed on a spatially flat FRW metric,
  <code>
  ds^2 = -dt^2 + a(t)^2 d\vec{x}^2
  </code>
- **Massless Gravity:** The graviton is assumed to be strictly massless throughout.
- **Perturbative Expansion:** Both classical and quantum (one-loop) perturbative analyses will be performed around the FRW background.
- **Explicit Construction:** All symmetry transformations will be explicitly constructed and their action on the Lagrangian demonstrated.

## Justification and Limitations

The focus on ghosts and tachyons is justified by their direct connection to the higher-derivative nature of DHOST theories and their potential to render the theory physically unacceptable. Other instabilities, while important, are secondary in the context of the present study. The analysis is limited to the FRW background and may not capture all possible pathologies in more general spacetimes.

## Requirement for Explicit Symmetry Construction

All candidate symmetries must be explicitly formulated as transformations of the variables in the Lagrangian, and their invariance properties must be demonstrated in the context of the FRW background. The compatibility of these symmetries with observational constraints will be explicitly checked.
