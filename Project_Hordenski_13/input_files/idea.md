**Title:** Symmetry versus Naturalness: A Case Study in Quantum-Corrected Scalar-Tensor Gravity

**Abstract:**

Protective symmetries are essential for the classical viability of scalar-tensor theories like DHOST, as they eliminate Ostrogradsky ghost instabilities. In any Effective Field Theory (EFT) framework, however, such symmetries are generically expected to be broken by quantum corrections arising from an unknown UV completion. This breaking reintroduces the ghost, creating a tension between classical stability and quantum consistency. This paper investigates the precise cost of maintaining such a symmetry in the presence of leading-order corrections.

We pose the question: what specific, non-generic form must the couplings of higher-derivative operators take for the protective symmetry to be preserved? We analyze the provided action, which includes Gauss-Bonnet and Weyl-squared terms, and first confirm that constant couplings explicitly violate the symmetry. Then, by promoting these couplings to functions of the scalar field and its kinetic term, $\beta_{\text{GB}}(\phi, X)$ and $\beta_{W^2}(\phi, X)$, we derive the conditions required for symmetry restoration.

We find that a unique, non-trivial solution for these functions exists, intrinsically linking the form of the quantum corrections to the classical theory's structure. A targeted Hamiltonian analysis confirms that this exceptionally symmetric action is, by construction, free of the Ostrogradsky ghost. However, we argue that this result should not be interpreted as a new guiding principle. Instead, it serves as a quantitative measure of a severe fine-tuning problem. The existence of this solution highlights the extreme fragility of the protective symmetry, as it requires the UV physics to conspire in a highly unnatural way to produce these exact functional forms. Our work thus frames the challenge for these models not as one of possibility, but of plausibility, pitting the elegance of symmetry against the EFT principle of naturalness.
