**Title:** The Renormalization Group Flow of Degeneracy: A Dynamical Test of Quantum Stability in Higher-Order Scalar-Tensor Gravity

**Abstract:** We propose to resolve the question of quantum stability for Type Ia degenerate scalar-tensor theories by shifting the paradigm from a search for protective symmetries to a direct analysis of the theory's dynamics in the space of effective field theories. The degeneracy conditions, which are crucial for classically exorcising the Ostrogradsky ghost, define an algebraic "healthy" subspace within the infinite-dimensional space of all possible theories parametrized by the functions $F(\phi, X)$, $A_1(\phi, X)$, and $A_3(\phi, X)$.

Our central thesis is that true quantum stability requires this healthy subspace to be a fixed manifold (or an attractor) under the Renormalization Group (RG) flow. Any RG trajectory that begins on this manifold but flows off it signifies that quantum corrections inevitably re-introduce the ghost, rendering the classical model an unstable fine-tuning.

To test this, we will employ the path integral formalism and the background field method to compute the full one-loop effective action and its associated UV-divergent counter-terms, $\mathcal{L}_{ct}$, for a generic theory lying on the degenerate manifold. From these counter-terms, we will derive the beta-functionals that govern the running of the defining functions $F, A_1,$ and $A_3$. The primary objective is to calculate the RG flow vector in the space of theories and explicitly determine whether it is tangent to the degeneracy manifold. A non-tangential flow would serve as a powerful "no-go" theorem, demonstrating the intrinsic quantum instability of this class of modified gravity theories, not due to the absence of a symmetry, but as a fundamental consequence of their quantum dynamics.
