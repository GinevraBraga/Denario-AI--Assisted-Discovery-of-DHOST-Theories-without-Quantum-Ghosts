We propose to unveil a novel *degeneracy-induced field-dependent gauge symmetry* inherent to the Type Ia DHOST subclass. This paper will first explicitly derive the general DHOST degeneracy condition and restrict to Type Ia, demonstrating its role in preventing classical instabilities. Crucially, we will then demonstrate that this very degeneracy condition *is equivalent to the requirement for* and *directly defines* a specific non-linear, field-dependent gauge symmetry. We will explicitly compute the transformation functions for this symmetry, showing how its gauge parameters are linked to the degenerate directions of the theory. Subsequently, we will derive the associated Ward identities, demonstrating how these identities impose stringent algebraic constraints on the DHOST functions and, critically, on the *structure of one-loop radiative counterterms*. This mechanism will be shown to protect the theory from radiative instabilities by preventing the generation of problematic terms, such as higher-derivative ghosts or destabilizing kinetic term renormalizations, at the one-loop level. Finally, we will investigate the persistence and implications of this symmetry—and its stabilizing mechanism—within the Horndeski and Beyond Horndeski limits, revealing a deeper connection between classical degeneracy and quantum robustness in generalized scalar-tensor theories.
