Begin with the general DHOST Lagrangian given in Eq. (2.8) of arXiv:1811.06271 and derive explicitly the corresponding degeneracy condition. Then, restrict the analysis to the Type Ia subclass defined in arXiv:1602.08398 and show in detail how the degeneracy condition prevents the appearance of classical instabilities, such as ghost or gradient modes, at the tree level. After establishing this, investigate whether the resulting Lagrangian possesses a hidden symmetry capable of protecting the theory from radiative instabilities at the one-loop level. Compute explicitly the transformation functions that generate this symmetry and determine how they constrain the functions appearing in the Lagrangian. Finally, examine whether this symmetry—and its stabilizing mechanism—remains valid when the Lagrangian reduces to the Horndeski and Beyond Horndeski limits.
