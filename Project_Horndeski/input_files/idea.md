**Quantum Kinetic Structure Preservation (QKSP) Symmetry for Horndeski Theories**

This paper proposes that "hidden symmetries" in Horndeski theories are defined by specific algebraic and differential relations among the $G_i$ functions that ensure a "Quantum Kinetic Structure Preservation (QKSP)" principle. This principle dictates that the intrinsic ghost-free kinetic structure of the theory, valid classically, is explicitly maintained at the one-loop level. The QKSP symmetries ensure that any higher-derivative terms generated in the one-loop effective action for the physical degrees of freedom (tensor and scalar modes) are precisely of a form that *do not introduce new propagating ghost degrees of freedom*. The analysis will involve explicitly deriving these QKSP conditions, demonstrating how they constrain the quantum effective action to preserve positive-definite kinetic terms for physical fluctuations, and thoroughly assessing their compatibility with observational constraints such as the speed of gravitational waves ($c_T^2=1$), cosmological expansion, and structure growth.
