**Quantum Stability Mechanisms in All-Order Stable Massive DHOST Gravity: A Foundational EFT Analysis and Massless Limit**

This paper presents a groundbreaking, rigorous theoretical investigation into the all-order stable massive DHOST theories identified in DHOSTS.pdf. We will perform a precise classification of these theories within the framework of arxiv.org/pdf/1608.08135, uniquely delineating their position and identifying the minimal set of conditions that guarantee their exceptional stability. The core novelty lies in a foundational theoretical analysis using effective field theory (EFT) principles to precisely explain *how* the interplay of graviton mass and specific symmetries ensures the absence of ghost instabilities and strong coupling at first-order perturbative loop corrections. This analysis will focus on identifying the specific mechanisms and structural properties within these DHOST theories that prevent problematic quantum corrections, providing a deep, principled understanding beyond general statements, without performing exhaustive, general loop computations. Furthermore, a comprehensive study of the massless graviton limit will reveal subtle conditions and potential branches for recovering known theories, critically examining the robustness of the limit and its implications for unifying massive and massless gravity frameworks.
