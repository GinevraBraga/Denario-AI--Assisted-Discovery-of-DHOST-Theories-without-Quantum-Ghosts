**Title:** The Hamiltonian Origin of Symmetry: Proving the Equivalence of Degeneracy and Gauge Invariance in Quantum-Corrected Scalar-Tensor Theories

**Description:**
This paper establishes a fundamental equivalence between two distinct approaches for ensuring the viability of a scalar-tensor Effective Field Theory (EFT) that includes higher-derivative quantum corrections. We begin with a general action combining a DHOST theory with the Gauss-Bonnet and Weyl-squared operators, whose coefficients, $\beta_{GB}$ and $\beta_{W^2}$, are functions of the scalar field $\phi$ and its kinetic term $X$.

Our investigation follows a two-pronged, parallel analysis:
1.  **The Symmetry Condition:** We first calculate the explicit breaking of the classical, degeneracy-enforcing gauge symmetry by the higher-derivative terms. By demanding that the full action remain invariant under this transformation, we derive a set of differential equations that the functions $\beta_{GB}(\phi, X)$ and $\beta_{W^2}(\phi, X)$ must satisfy. This represents the algebraic condition for the theory to respect the protective symmetry.
2.  **The Degeneracy Condition:** Independently, we perform a first-principles Hamiltonian analysis of the full action. By imposing the requirement that the theory be free of the Ostrogradsky ghost, we derive the set of primary and secondary constraints that must be satisfied, which again translates into a set of differential equations for $\beta_{GB}(\phi, X)$ and $\beta_{W^2}(\phi, X)$. This is the dynamical condition for the health of the theory.

The central result of this work is the rigorous proof that these two sets of conditions are identical. We demonstrate that the algebraic requirement of restoring a gauge symmetry is the exact counterpart to the dynamical requirement of ghost-elimination from the Hamiltonian perspective. This equivalence provides a powerful new insight—the classical protective symmetry is not broken by quantum corrections but must be extended to them—and offers a significant practical advantage, establishing the simpler symmetry analysis as a reliable tool for constructing consistent EFTs without the need for a full, and often intractable, Hamiltonian calculation.
