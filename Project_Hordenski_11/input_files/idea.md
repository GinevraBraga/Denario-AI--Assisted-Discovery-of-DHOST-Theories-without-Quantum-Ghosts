**Title:** A No-Go Theorem from the Anomaly: Quantum Instability in Symmetric DHOST Gravity

**Abstract:** The classical stability of Degenerate Higher-Order Scalar-Tensor (DHOST) theories, guaranteed by degeneracy conditions that eliminate ghost degrees of freedom, is not necessarily preserved at the quantum level. To investigate this in a controlled setting, we first isolate a tractable and physically motivated subclass of Type Ia DHOST theories. Instead of postulating a symmetry for all theories, we derive the mathematical integrability conditions that the free functions ($F, A_1, A_3$) must satisfy to admit the specific field-dependent gauge symmetry defined by the transformations $\delta_\epsilon \phi = \epsilon \Lambda(\phi, X)$ and $\delta_\epsilon g_{\mu\nu} = \epsilon \mathcal{L}_{\xi} g_{\mu\nu}$ with $\xi^\mu = \alpha(\phi, X) \nabla^\mu\phi$. This procedure identifies a non-trivial class of models that possess this symmetry at the classical level.

Focusing on the simplest such symmetric model, we then analyze its quantum properties. The central thesis of this paper is to test the fate of this classical symmetry and its connection to the theory's stability. Using the path integral formalism and the background field method, we compute the one-loop effective action. We demonstrate that the classical symmetry is broken by quantum effects, i.e., it is anomalous. Our key result is the explicit calculation of the divergent counter-terms comprising the anomaly. We show that these terms have a structure that precisely violates the original degeneracy conditions of the theory.

This result constitutes a specific no-go theorem: for this class of theories, the very quantum effects that break the classical symmetry are responsible for reintroducing the Ostrogradsky ghost. This establishes a concrete link between anomaly generation and the loss of quantum stability, suggesting that the requirement of anomaly cancellation could serve as a powerful new selection principle for constructing viable theories of modified gravity.
